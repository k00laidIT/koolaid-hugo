As a modern social media based society we've found it very easy to do movements, experiments, and concepts in marketing-approved monthly form. No shave November. Blogtober. Hacktoberfest. So on and so on. Here of late I've noticed that I am leaning way too hard into the the whole brain rot of it all and thought I'd roll my own version of this concept; Offline October, where I delete all the social media apps from my devices and stop doom scrolling as a way to pass the time.

## Oh no, why?

In case you haven't noticed the state of discourse at least here in the USA has got a little crazy. There's no room for legitimate comparison of facts, it's just flame bait from all angles. Social media with all it's bots and algorithms, feeding into our own already felt beliefs only makes this worse. I've found that at least for myself I'm either spending too much of my life angry about things that I cannot control or just mindlessly scrolling through 27 part Reddit stories designed to piss me off and ain't nobody got time for that.

I'll be honest and say that there's also an aspect of parenting playing into this. I want to be a better example to my teenage daughter when I tell her that being terminally online is not good for you by not saying that as I personally endlessly scroll Instagram or BlueSky.

## How's life going to be living under a rock?

This is not to say that I will be completely removed from what's going on in the world and disconnected from reality. Let's be honest, the vast majority of what we see that is related to current events on social media is either fake, extreme, or targeted to be devisive. Instead I will be getting my news the old fashioned way, from legitimate news sources. Further as I work remotely in tech I cannot be completely offline, Slack and you know, human beings, will still be there.

There will also be a professional aspect of social media that I can't avoid. Slack for work is an absolutely necessity but while at this point I'm not going to leave any of the more general purpose workspaces I may in the future. See also Discord even though I use that far less. This will mainly be via LinkedIn but things like post sharing will still happen to all my usual channels/accounts. The irony of this post being spread to LinkedIn, Facebook, Threads, BlueSky, Mastadon, etc. is not lost on me. ;)

## So what will you be doing with your time?

This is the part I am actually most looking forward to. I find that the more brain rot I allow into my life the less I am capable of long form, well, anything. I want to start picking up some physical, printed on paper books and read those. For example I've been working my way through the Harvard Business Review Series on [Emotional Intelligence](https://www.amazon.com/HBR-Emotional-Intelligence-Boxed-Books/dp/1633696219). I've also picked up a new copy of [The Cuckoo's Egg](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18154.The_Cuckoo_s_Egg) and [The Phoenix Project,](https://www.amazon.com/Phoenix-Project-DevOps-Helping-Business/dp/0988262509) 2 of my favorite books much overdue for re-reads. There is a good deal of good movies and television to pass my time (I'm looking right at you season 3 of [The Diplomat](https://www.netflix.com/title/81288983)).

I also want/need to start getting to a deeper level of understanding of some tech things as well. As the VMware world moves to VMware Cloud Foundation I need to be moving with it so I'll be working through a bunch of training there over the next 3 months or so. Further as I'll be speaking at [SecureWV](https://www.securewv.org/) on October 24-25 I should probably get to work putting my content together.

## When will this madness end?

In theory and in spirit of any monthly thing this SHOULD end when October ends but with all things there's any number of variables to that. If this goes the way I want it to I may extend it indefinitely or selectively bring some platforms back in while minimizing the platforms that really do not provide value other than time killers. So like all things, "it depends."

If you do need to reach me there are always ways. Email for one is right there (even though, ewww) but I'll still monitor direct messaging on LinkedIn, Reddit and Facebook. In any case unless you need me, I'll see you in a month!
