Ok, now that the AI generated content is out of the way let's talk about the fact that this is site is once again on the move, this time to the [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/) platform. This is a fairly popular move right now and for me it was inspired by finding the excellent work of [Matt Broberg](https://mbbroberg.fun/) as he continues his public learning and living. Along the way there's been a lot of discussion with others and in part [Tim Smith](https://tsmith.co) and I have worked our way through this in very similar ways. You can read more about his [journey from WordPress to Hugo](https://tsmith.co/2026/migrating-15-years-of-wordpress-to-hugo-with-ai/) and probably see a lot of similarities to what you see here. 

For me this is just the latest version of the journey. In the process of migrating data over to here I found the post about [moving to WordPress](posts/wordpress-come/) from way back in 2014 so it's been a minute. Before that was Drupal, before that some old ASP slop but long story short this site and other variants have existed since 2003!

## So Why Move?
There are a few reasons why I decided to move my site at this time. I've recently been doing a lot of work migrating digital brain into [markdown](https://www.markdownguide.org/), mostly through [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md). If you aren't familiar Markdown is everywhere you are probably working, you just don't know it because it's masked in a GUI wrapper. As someone who has always been a "just give me a keyboard shortcut" type this definitely speaks to me. 

Second was the learning aspect of it all. Hugo is reliant on git and usually github, technologies I've used for years but never really dove deep into so I've got to learn a great deal about that in this process. Another educational driver for this was a need/want to learn more about AI assisted development/IT work. I love to learn with a purpose but not one that will effect my work so "hey, let's refactor my website" absolutely fit the bill. Virtually everything you see in terms of layout as well as publication of this site has been coworked with Anthropic's Claude Code. It's never been allowed to just autonomously work but it's truly been like working with someone who is capable of fast, analytic work. 

{{< figure src="claude-screenshot.png" alt="screenshot of a claude answer to stupid questions" class="left" >}}
An example of this  the actual act of migrating images and content over proved to be an absolutely pain in the ... but I was able to point claude at both the development and my production site and tell it to compare images and ensure that images were brought over and formatted correctly into the pages. While yes, I could have done this myself what would have been doing so for 151 posts would have taken days by hands, less than an hour when assisted. 

Finally the latest corporate choices by [Automattic](https://automattic.com/) and their founder, [Matt Mullenweg](https://ma.tt/about/?ref=onemanandhisblog.com) definitely played a part in my decision to leave the platform/ecosystem. While it's not for me to recount completely here there is plenty of readiing to be had on it: 
- https://www.theverge.com/decoder-podcast-with-nilay-patel/693052/automattic-ceo-matt-mullenweg-wordpress-drama-wp-engine-open-source
- https://onemanandhisblog.com/2024/03/what-the-hell-is-happening-at-automattic/
- https://onemanandhisblog.com/2024/09/what-the-hell-is-happening-at-automattic-part-2/
Look, I am an American and as a byproduct of that I'm a fan of Capitalism and recognize that for every good idea is somebody looking to make money off of it. That said if you build your world around a product that you released into the wild you can't get upset when others also want to make money on it and try to claw it back. That's how open source works.

## Conclusion
In the end this should be a fun little project to keep me interested in creating content. I've loved doing this for years, I hope I keep doing so. I'm sure as you search and find this site you'll find where some things didn't get ported over correctly and if you do I'd love for you to reach out via any of the social links below. Have fun and happy blogging all!
