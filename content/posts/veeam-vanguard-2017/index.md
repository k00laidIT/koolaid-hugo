It has been a great day here because today I learned that I have once again been awarded acceptance into the excellent [Veeam Vanguard](https://www.veeam.com/vanguard.html) program, my third time. This program, above any others that I am or have been involved with takes a more personal approach to creating a group of awardees who not only deserve anything good they get out of it but give back just as much to the community itself. In only its 3rd year the group has grown; from 31 the first year, 50(ish) the second, to a total of 62 this year. There are 21 new awardees in that 62 number so there really isn't a rubber stamp to stay included, it is legitimately awarded each year. The group has grown each year but as you can see not by the leaps and bounds others have, and for good reason. There is no way this experience could be had with a giant community. At this point in the post I would typically tell you a bit about what the Vanguard program is and isn't but honestly, Veeam's own [Dmitry Kniazev](http://www.vmdk.co/) really put it best in a couple recent posts, "[*Veeam Vanguard Part 1: WTH Is This*](http://www.vmdk.co/2017/01/veeam-vanguard-part-1-wth/)?" and "*[Veeam Vanguard Part 2: What It's Not](http://www.vmdk.co/2017/01/veeam-vanguard-part-2-not/)."*  What I will add is that as nice as some of the perks are, as DK says in the Part 1 post the true perk is the intangibles; a vibrant community full of some of the smartest, most passionate people in the industry and in many cases access right to the people approving and disapproving changes to their software. These are the thing that made me sweat approval time. Once again I would give a giant thank you to Veeam Software and especially the whole Vanguard crew. This includes [Rick Vanover](http://twitter.com/rickvanover), [Clint Wyckoff](http://twitter.com/clintwyckoff), [Michael White](http://twitter.com/mwVme), [Michael Cade](http://twitter.com/michaelcade1), [Anthony Spiteri](http://twitter.com/anthonyspiteri), [Kirsten Stoner](http://twitter.com/kstoner), [Dmitry Kniazev](http://twitter.com/vmdk_co), [Andrew Zhelezko](http://twitter.com/andrewzhelezko) and finally [Doug Hazelman](http://twitter.com/vmdoug). Without these people it wouldn't be nearly as nice.
